Defining different mental illnesses

Mental illness is a very delicate topic to discuss. It is extremely important that you do not diagnose yourself or others based of off information you learn online. Instead, use these resources if you believe you may have an mental illness and then go to a psychologist, therapist, or any professional in the field for a diagnosis. The most common mental illnesses are anxiety disorder. There are mental health disorders characterized by feelings of worry, anxiety, or fear that are strong enough to interfere with one's daily activities. Teenagers and the role of mental health in their lives

Adolescence is a crucial period for developing and maintaining social and emotional habits that are important for mental well-being. These include adopting healthy sleep habits, taking regular exercise, developing coping mechanisms, problem-solving, and interpersonal skills, and learning to manage emotions. Supportive family, school, and community environments are also important. An estimated 10–20% of adolescents globally experience mental health issues, yet these remain underdiagnosed and undertreated. The more risk factors adolescents are exposed to, the greater the potential impact on their mental health is. Factors that can contribute to stress during adolescence include pressure to conform with peers, exploration of sexual identity, and increased access to and use of technology.
